Mole-Day Facts!
  • Did you know that the National Mole Day Foundation was created on May 15, 1991? That means it's over 23 years old!

  • The seeds for the celebration of Mole Day were planted when an article appeared in early 1980's magazine The Science Teacher about a high school chemistry teacher wanting to celebrate Mole Day!

  • The "mole" unit itself was invented in the 18th century, by Amadeo Avagadro; his full name is "Lorenzo Romano Amedeo Carlo Avogadro di Queregna e di Cerreto". He stated that equal volumes of all gasses, at the same temperature and pressure, have the same number of molecules.

  • Each year, Mole day is celebrated on October 23rd (10/23) from 6:02 AM to 6:02 PM. To maximize your Mole-Day, first fly to Japan, celebrate Mole Day there, and once it's close to ending, fly to California!

  • There are two well-known Mole Day pledges: "I pledge allegiance to the mole, and to the science from which it comes, one SI unit, extremely divisible, with micromoles and millimoles for all.", and "I pledge allegiance to the mole, to the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry, and to the atomic mass for which it stands, one number, most divisible, with atoms and molecules for all."

  • If you stack one mole of paper, it would reach the moon and back at least 80 billion times! A mole of marbles could cover the entire surface of the Earth, 10 feet deep.
